Welcome to Sharanya! I would like to thank you in advance for your commitment to dance.
To allow the dance school to function smoothly, I ask that you follow these guidelines:


Attendance

Students are expected to attend all classes. At times when a student must miss a class, please be courteous and inform me in advance. Because I am invested in the progress of my students and find that “keeping momentum” is invaluable, I ask that students take responsibility to schedule a make up class with me on the designated day of that month. Likewise, in case classes have to be rescheduled on my behalf, I will do so in advance.


Transportation for Students

  1. Parents should drop off and pick up their children from the house garage entrance. If anyone else, other than the parent or guardian, will be picking up your child on a specific day, please notify me in advance.
  2. Please avoid waiting inside during your child’s class session as this can be distracting for the class. However, if parents would like to observe class for any reason, please notify in advance so that arrangements can be made.

Classes and Curriculum

  1. Students are required to practice at home. For younger students, a “Dance Practice Log” will be updated after each class so the students will know what to practice. Parents should sign off that the student has practice so that progress can be tracked. Students are responsible for bringing their binders to each class.
  2. Worksheets and materials will be provided to facilitate the learning process. Please understand that this material is meant for Sharanya classes only and should not be shared or distributed to others.
  3. Music will also be provided, if needed, for the students to practice. Please note that all music is for practice purposes only.
  4. Students are encouraged to seek opportunities to perform. As students complete the fundamentals and advance, traditional Bharatanatyam pieces will be taught in class. Please notify me in advance if you would like for your student to perform any pieces outside of Sharanya events. I would be more than happy to discuss which piece would work best for the function and help prepare the student for the event.
  5. Non-classical pieces, incorporating classical basis, can be taught outside of class upon request to provide students with materials for performances at social functions. Please note that this is preferred after the student has a basic foundatino in Bharatanatyam.

Fees

Fees are requested promptly at the beginning of each calendar session, usually a 3-month quarter. Parents will be notified in advance how much the fees for each quarter will be depending on the schedule. Fees may vary with sessions and are subject to change with student’s progress and involvement. Late payments can be charged a late fee.

General Guidelines

  1. Students, parents and visitors are asked to not wear shoes on the dance floor.
  2. Students should not eat food or candy during class sessions unless provided by the teacher.
  3. Students are asked to address older peers as Didi, Baihem/Bhaiya or Akka/Annan. Students may refer to me as Didi. Parents can refer to me by my first name.
  4. Even though a residence is used as an institution, please respect the privacy of a home with care for furniture and its belongings. Therefore students, students’ siblings, and parents should not roam around the house or talk if they are in the studio.
  5. Students should come to class in a traditional Salwar Kameez, Kurta Pajama or Dance Sari. Leggings and a long Kurta top are acceptable. Students should avoid wearing jeans, tight clothes, shorts or short skirts. Students will be asked to sit aside if they are not in acceptable practice attire. Older students are required to wear a Dupatta to tie around their waist or across their torso. Hair should be neatly tied back.

Again, if you would like to clarify any points feel free to contact me at sharanyadance@gmail.com or at (240) 257-2744.

Thank you once more for your commitment to Sharanya!

Sincerely,
Rashi Narain
Director
